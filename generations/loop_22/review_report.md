# Loop 22 Cellular Automata Visual Review: Quantum Decoherence & Wavefunction Collapse

This report provides a detailed aesthetic, structural, and complexity analysis of the 20 space-time diagrams generated in **Loop 22**. The domain focus of this loop is **Quantum Decoherence & Wavefunction Collapse**, modeling quantum state superpositions, measurement-induced collapse, tunneling, phase transitions, and entanglement under environmental noise.

---

## 1. Loop Summary & Domain Focus
Loop 22 explores cellular automata (CA) rules designed to simulate quantum phenomena. The dynamics are inherently probabilistic, capturing the transition from coherent quantum states (often represented as expanding wave structures) to localized, classical states (represented by narrow lines or decay products). 

A key theme in this loop is **spatial boundary interaction** and **scale sensitivity**. Several rules introduce static features (potential barriers, measurement erasers, Majorana qubit wires) to study how wave packets interact with them. 

---

## 2. Rule-by-Rule Visual Analysis

### Rule 1: Spontaneous Localization (GRW Collapse)
* **Single-Seed:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* A highly symmetric and delicate pattern. It starts with a cyan triangular cap (state 1, coherent wave packet) that suddenly collapses into vertical red and purple lines (states 2 and 3, spin-up and spin-down). These vertical lines drip downwards like neon rain or icicles, gradually fading away (decaying back to vacuum, state 0).
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Transitions from orderly wave expansion (cyan) to localized particle collapse (red/purple), ending in a quiet vacuum.
  * *Aesthetics:* Poetic and clean. The high contrast of the neon colors against the dark slate blue background is exceptionally striking.
* **Random:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* A dense fringe of cyan wave packets along the top, with a massive curtain of vertical red and purple drips. Within the first 250 steps, the entire lattice relaxes back to the vacuum.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Starts chaotic but quickly settles into total thermodynamic order (vacuum).
  * *Aesthetics:* Rich and organic, resembling a colorful stalactite cave formation.

### Rule 2: Quantum Tunneling through Barriers
* **Single-Seed:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* Three thin, vertical grey lines represent potential barriers. Diagonal cyan (right-moving) and orange (left-moving) lines collide with the barriers and reflect perfectly, forming a herringbone or chevron pattern.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Highly ordered, periodic, and deterministic in appearance. No tunneling actually occurs, leaving the regions between barriers completely black.
  * *Aesthetics:* Clean and geometric, but statically empty in the middle.
* **Random:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* A few diagonal lines at the top that reflect off barriers and quickly run out, leaving the lower 90% of the image dark.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Dissipates rapidly into static empty space.
  * *Aesthetics:* Sparse and underwhelming.

### Rule 3: Environmental Decoherence Phase Transition
* **Single-Seed:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* A single, massive green triangle (coherent state) expanding at a 45-degree angle. The surrounding vacuum is dotted with sparse red noise dots.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Complete dominance of the coherent phase. The interior is noise-free due to the update rules.
  * *Aesthetics:* Extremely simple and blocky.
* **Random:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* A solid green block filling 99% of the image.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Homogeneous order (fully coherent phase).
  * *Aesthetics:* Visually flat and uninteresting.

### Rule 4: Quantum Zeno & Anti-Zeno Dynamics
* **Single-Seed:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* A spectacular geometric layout. A central yellow-and-black textured V-shape sits at the top, flanked by massive, solid triangles of neon cyan (left) and hot pink (right). A jagged grey/blue path separates them at the bottom.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Captures a beautiful tension. The yellow region is highly chaotic/textured, while the cyan and pink regions are perfectly solid and frozen due to the Zeno effect.
  * *Aesthetics:* The highest-contrast and most visually arresting image in the loop. The bold blocks of neon color combined with the central textured wedge feel like modern pop art.
* **Random:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* Thick vertical bands of solid cyan and pink with highly organic, jagged grey boundaries. Tiny yellow V-shaped noise wedges are visible at the top.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Strongly partitioned, showing how localized Zeno measurements lock the lattice into macro-scale phases.
  * *Aesthetics:* A masterpiece of computational abstraction, resembling a screen-printed fabric or a bold digital painting.

### Rule 5: EPR Entanglement and Causal Collapse
* **Single-Seed:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* A central white line (source) emits diagonal cyan and blue lines. Once a measurement occurs, it triggers a "causal collapse wave" that retro-propagates and forces the opposite side to collapse into the opposite spin. This creates massive blocks of green and orange with a fine diagonal pinstripe texture.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Highly structured. The diagonal texture adds a level of complexity and depth that makes the solid blocks feel active.
  * *Aesthetics:* Extremely clean, balanced, and vector-like.
* **Random:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* Seven vertical white lines (sources) flanked by thick columns of orange and green, with diagonal propagation trails at the top.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* High macro-scale structure, clearly demonstrating non-local correlations.
  * *Aesthetics:* Bold, graphic, and highly structured.

### Rule 6: Quantum Wave Walk & Phase Interference
* **Single-Seed:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* A single central vertical strand of cyan and violet pixels that fluctuates slightly and drifts, looking like a thin string or flame.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Highly localized. Waves self-interfere and cancel out before they can expand.
  * *Aesthetics:* Very narrow and sparse; most of the canvas is empty dark indigo.
* **Random:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* A few brief waves at the top that quickly collapse to yellow lines or cancel out, leaving a dark empty screen.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Rapid collapse to vacuum.
  * *Aesthetics:* Empty and dark.

### Rule 7: Dissipative Decoherence in a Thermal Bath
* **Single-Seed:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* A dense field of deep blue background, continuously punctured by yellow thermal sparks that decay into red and then dark grey streaks.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Highly chaotic and dynamic, resembling vertical rain or digital noise.
  * *Aesthetics:* A rich, textured fabric-like quality, but lacks macroscopic geometric shapes.
* **Random:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* Hyper-dense, vertical static noise of blue, black, orange, and red.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Homogeneous chaos.
  * *Aesthetics:* Visually overwhelming, resembling a television screen tuned to static.

### Rule 8: Penrose Gravitational Self-Energy Collapse
* **Single-Seed:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* A single vertical yellow line at the center on a dark background.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Total collapse into a single classical mass line.
  * *Aesthetics:* Extremely minimal and empty.
* **Random:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* A few vertical yellow lines on a dark background.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Highly sparse and simple.
  * *Aesthetics:* Lacks complexity.

### Rule 9: Topological Majorana Protection
* **Single-Seed:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* Three thick vertical golden bands on a noisy purple background.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Static gold bands with uniform background noise.
  * *Aesthetics:* Simple and static.
  * > [!NOTE]
    > **Implementation Detail:** A neighborhood indexing mismatch in the nucleation condition `wire_end_left` and `wire_end_right` causes Majorana modes (cyan) to never spawn on wires of width > 1. As a result, the gold wires remain completely static and never interact with the surrounding noise.
* **Random:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* Seven thick vertical golden bands on a noisy purple background.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Static and uniform.

### Rule 10: Delayed-Choice Quantum Eraser
* **Single-Seed:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* Two thick vertical orange bands (erasers) with solid green regions (marked paths) on the outer sides, and a black region in the middle.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Static, blocky phases.
  * *Aesthetics:* Simple and flat.
  * > [!NOTE]
    > **Implementation Detail:** Similar to Rule 9, the emergence condition checks `left_2 == 1` (state 1 must be exactly adjacent to the outer boundary of the eraser). Since the erasers are 40 cells wide, state 1 never tunnels/emerges as state 2 (sky blue) on the inside, and no interference peaks (hot pink) can form.
* **Random:** 
  * *Visual Structure:* Alternating orange and green vertical bands.
  * *Order vs. Chaos:* Simple and blocky.
