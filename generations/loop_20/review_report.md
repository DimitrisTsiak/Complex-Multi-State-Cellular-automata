# Loop 20 Review Report: Non-Euclidean Wormholes & Spatial Folding

## 1. Summary of the Loop and Domain Focus

Loop 20 focuses on the domain of **Non-Euclidean Wormholes & Spatial Folding**. The cellular automata rules in this loop explore alternative spatial topologies, non-local shortcuts, coordinate folding, and multidimensional projections. These include portal teleportation, Klein bottle mirror folding, hypercubic coordinate shifts, metric wormhole routing, Möbius boundary twists, variable metric gravitational lensing, chaotic portal percolation, 2D projected toroidal solitons, and quantum entanglement bridges.

The primary objective is to investigate how non-local spatial connectivity, boundary transformations, and variable metrics alter the propagation of information, wave patterns, and chaos. Spacetime diagrams generated in this loop show how these topological modifications create unique visual signatures—ranging from clean teleportation tracks to complex woven lattices, fractal cones, and globally synchronized states.

## 2. Visual Analysis of the 10 Rules

### Rule 1: Portal Pair Teleportation
* **Mechanism:** Radius $r=1$ local neighborhood plus a non-local diametric partner coordinate $x^* = (x + W/2) \bmod W$. Left- and right-moving packets collide to spawn portal pairs that absorb subsequent packets and teleport them to the other side.
* **Single-Seed Initialization:** Highly ordered and sparse. A single collision creates a pair of horizontal portal mouths. Moving packets are absorbed and reappear at the partner portal, creating symmetrical, offset diagonal tracks that eventually exit the boundaries. The lattice then settles into a clean vacuum (state 0).
* **Random Initialization:** A scattering of diagonal tracks that form portal pairs and teleport. Because there is no continuous source of packets, the lattice rapidly empties as particles exit the boundaries or get absorbed by the portals. The final state is a dark vacuum with static horizontal lines where portals remain.

### Rule 2: Klein Bottle Spatial Folding
* **Mechanism:** Radius $r=1$ local neighborhood with boundary cells connected as mirror partners $x' = W - 1 - x$, reversing the parity/direction of propagating particles ($1 \leftrightarrow 2$).
* **Single-Seed Initialization:** Clean, sparse diagonal lines. Particles strike the fold zones at the boundaries and emerge on the mirror side with reversed chirality. Since no new particles are generated, the activity quickly decays, leaving a dark vacuum.
* **Random Initialization:** Sparse diagonal wavefronts that reflect and flip slopes at the boundaries. The system rapidly relaxes into a quiet state as particles annihilate or exit.

### Rule 3: Dynamic Hypercube Projection
* **Mechanism:** Radius $r=1$ local neighborhood plus a dynamically shifted coordinate $x_{hyper} = (x \oplus \theta_t) \bmod W$, where $\theta_t$ is determined by the global state sum.
* **Single-Seed Initialization:** Exhibits an expanding cone with step-like boundaries. The region outside the cone is filled with globally synchronized green-blue horizontal stripes. Within the cone, the global feedback coupled with XOR routing produces a highly chaotic, high-entropy fractal noise.
* **Random Initialization:** A uniform, fine-grained static noise pattern with very high entropy. The global synchronization creates faint horizontal phase shifts, but it is dominated by local chaos.

### Rule 4: Metric Wormhole Routing
* **Mechanism:** Packets move along geodesic paths using a fixed portal shortcut $x_E \to x_X$ toward a gravitational beacon.
* **Single-Seed Initialization:** Shows three vertical lines representing the fixed portal entrance, exit, and beacon. Diagonal light-blue tracks enter the entrance, vanish, and instantly reappear at the exit, continuing to the beacon. A very clean and clear visualization of non-local metric routing.
* **Random Initialization:** Multiple diagonal tracks routing through the portal exit. Since it is a one-way deterministic routing, the grid eventually stabilizes into simple periodic attractors or empties.

### Rule 5: Non-Euclidean Thermal Wormhole
* **Mechanism:** Local heat diffusion coupled with a dynamic wormhole bridging the absolute maximum and minimum state coordinates at each step.
* **Single-Seed Initialization:** A highly complex and visually stunning pattern. It features a central nested triangular core (dark triangles with vibrant blue borders resembling a Sierpinski structure). The background is filled with a dense, highly textured noise of purple, pink, and orange. The top forms a large black V-shape.
* **Random Initialization:** A dense, uniform texture of purplish-pink, orange, and blue pixels. Shows high complexity and chaotic mixing but lacks macroscopic structure under random initialization.

### Rule 6: Möbius Boundary Twist
* **Mechanism:** Local neighborhood where spatial wrapping at boundaries shifts the state value by $+3 \pmod 6$ (a half-rotation in state space), forcing two full traversals to return to phase.
* **Single-Seed Initialization:** Outstanding geometric structure. It has a central nested triangle design with yellow and green vertical stripes. The wavefronts expand, hit the boundaries, and wrap around with a phase shift, creating symmetric side cones that intersect and create beautiful crystalline patterns.
* **Random Initialization:** An exquisite woven digital tapestry of yellow, green, and blue pixels. It is cut by diagonal lines of red/pink representing defect propagation, showcasing classic Class IV complex cellular automata behavior.

### Rule 7: Variable Metric Gravitational Lensing
* **Mechanism:** Local metric step size varies dynamically based on gravitational potential, bending wave paths near a stationary singularity (state 4).
* **Single-Seed Initialization:** Extremely simple. Features a white background with a few vertical stripes in the center and diagonal boundary lines separating black and white regions at the top. The lensing effect doesn't show much visual complexity.
* **Random Initialization:** Simple diagonal lines and vertical lines. Mostly uniform regions, suggesting the lensing dynamics quickly lead to absorption or simple periodic attractors.

### Rule 8: Chaotic Portal Percolation
* **Mechanism:** A local update that establishes a non-local link to a partner cell offset determined by the cell's state, but only when the state exceeds a threshold ($\ge 12$).
* **Single-Seed Initialization:** Creates a symmetric expanding cone of fine purple-pink noise. The noise is very uniform, without clear macroscopic features or nested structures.
* **Random Initialization:** Dense, uniform purple-pink noise across the entire space-time diagram, indicating high entropy chaos.

### Rule 9: 2D Projected Toroidal Solitons
* **Mechanism:** Projecting a 2D toroidal lattice onto a 1D line, mapping local 2D updates to offsets of $\pm 1$ and $\pm L_1$ in 1D.
* **Single-Seed Initialization:** Highly structured and unique. It projects a 2D toroidal lattice onto a 1D line, resulting in 20 vertical bands of textured noise, crossed by regular horizontal striations. It looks like a beautiful woven knit fabric or a digital textile.
* **Random Initialization:** The vertical columns are still clearly visible as bands of color variation in the noise, showing how the toroidal projection imposes a strong grid structure even with random inputs.

### Rule 10: Quantum Entanglement Bridges
* **Mechanism:** Pairs of cells at $x$ and $W - 1 - x$ share an entangled state. When a classical disruption wave measurements one cell, it collapses and causes an instantaneous, non-local collapse and spin-flip at its partner.
* **Single-Seed Initialization:** Large golden-yellow blocks of color, with a central cyan V-shape at the top and black triangles. Very clean, showing quick collapse of entangled states to the ground state.
* **Random Initialization:** Similar to the single-seed, with a few extra vertical cyan stripes. The system quickly decays to the golden-yellow ground state.

## 3. Selection of the Top 3 Winner Rules

The top 3 winner rules of Loop 20 have been selected based on their aesthetic appeal, structural complexity, balance of order and chaos, and how effectively they visualize non-Euclidean topology.

### Winner 1: Rule 6 (Möbius Boundary Twist)
* **Aesthetic Appeal:** Exceptional. Under single-seed initialization, the nested triangles are extremely clean, and the yellow-green striped texture gives it a high-resolution, retro-futuristic look. Under random initialization, it creates a gorgeous digital textile weave, interrupted by beautiful red and pink diagonal particle-like tracks.
* **Complexity:** High. The Möbius twist boundary conditions force a phase shift that creates interlocking side cones in the single seed, and complex Class IV particle/defect dynamics in the random initialization.
* **Why it stands out:** It bridges the gap between clean geometry and complex texture. The transition at the boundaries is visually clear and beautiful.

### Winner 2: Rule 5 (Non-Euclidean Thermal Wormhole)
* **Aesthetic Appeal:** Very high. The single-seed diagram is a masterpiece of complex textures. The dark inverted triangles with glowing cyan borders create a striking contrast against the highly detailed, multi-colored noise (purple, pink, orange).
* **Complexity:** Extremely High. The dynamic linking of absolute maximum and minimum states creates instantaneous thermal wormholes that act as a self-regulating energy balancer. This produces a unique mixture of fractal nested structures and high-entropy background textures.
* **Why it stands out:** The combination of a Sierpinski-like fractal core and a rich, colored noise background is visually arresting and represents a perfect blend of order and chaos.

### Winner 3: Rule 9 (2D Projected Toroidal Solitons)
* **Aesthetic Appeal:** Very high. It produces a regular, grid-like pattern that resembles woven fabric or a digital rug. The distribution of vertical bands and horizontal lines is highly satisfying and symmetric.
* **Complexity:** High. The projection of a 2D toroidal lattice onto a 1D coordinate system naturally generates periodic non-local shortcuts. This constraints the chaotic noise into highly structured vertical columns.
* **Why it stands out:** It is a perfect demonstration of dimensional folding. The visual signature immediately tells the story of a higher-dimensional space mapped onto 1D spacetime.
