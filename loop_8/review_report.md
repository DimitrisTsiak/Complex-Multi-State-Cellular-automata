# Loop 8 Cellular Automata Review Report: Quantum-Walk & Wave Interference

This report analyzes the space-time diagrams of 10 cellular automata (CA) rules developed in **Loop 8**, representing simulations of **Quantum-Walk & Wave Interference**. 

These rules simulate quantum-like behaviors (superposition, interference, tunneling, edge state conservation, and optical circuitry) on a 1D lattice of width $W = 800$ over $T = 800$ time steps. The space-time diagrams are plotted with time running downwards.

---

## Comprehensive Analysis of Rules

### Rule 1: Discrete Hadamard Walk
*   **Key Mechanism**: A sign-preserving Hadamard coin that models wave amplitudes with discrete signs.
*   **Visual Observations**: 
    *   **Single-Seed**: Forms a perfect, expanding symmetric cone. The left half is characterized by dense orange/red diagonal stripes, while the right half features blue/cyan stripes. The interior is filled with intricate interference fringes.
    *   **Random-Seed**: Produces a rich, woven, fabric-like texture of overlapping diagonal wave-packets.
*   **Dynamics**: Exhibits clean phase-dependent cancellation and reinforcement (constructive/destructive interference) at intersections.
*   **Aesthetic Value**: Excellent. The single-seed cone has a holographic, fine-grained look, while the random-seed texture is dense and detailed.

### Rule 2: Quantum Tunneling & Scattering
*   **Key Mechanism**: Wave-splitting at semi-permeable potential barriers (partial reflection and transmission) and full reflection at high potential walls.
*   **Visual Observations**: 
    *   **Single-Seed**: Shows a blue wave moving right, hitting a grey barrier, reflecting as a green wave, and partially tunneling through.
    *   **Random-Seed**: Displays a maze-like array of compartments separated by vertical grey and white barriers. The green and blue waves bounce back and forth, forming geometric grid cells.
*   **Dynamics**: Strictly diagonal propagation (speed 1) combined with static vertical barriers.
*   **Aesthetic Value**: Very good. It looks like a clean physical schematic of particles trapped in potential wells or quantum scattering.

### Rule 3: Discrete Wave Equation (Winner)
*   **Key Mechanism**: A discretized displacement-velocity coupled wave equation.
*   **Visual Observations**: 
    *   **Single-Seed**: Fills the entire light cone with a solid, uniform orange triangle.
    *   **Random-Seed**: Creates a striking, rectilinear plaid or tartan fabric pattern.
*   **Dynamics**: Intersecting wavefronts create flat, rectangular color patches (cyan, orange, purple, magenta) separated by sharp, crossing lines.
*   **Aesthetic Value**: Outstanding. The random-seed tartan pattern is visually stunning and has a powerful retro-digital textile quality.

### Rule 4: Decohering Quantum Walk
*   **Key Mechanism**: Coherent waves undergo decoherence and turn into classical noise upon hitting noise blocks.
*   **Visual Observations**: 
    *   **Single-Seed**: Two diagonal waves (cyan, purple) hit a central vertical orange noise block and are completely absorbed.
    *   **Random-Seed**: A sparse barcode of vertical orange lines on a black background, with tiny transient diagonals at the top.
*   **Dynamics**: Highly dissipative; the noise blocks act as sinks that quickly absorb the coherent wavefronts.
*   **Aesthetic Value**: Poor. The images are very sparse and mostly black.

### Rule 5: Bohmian Pilot-Wave CA
*   **Key Mechanism**: A continuous wave field that guides localized tracer particles.
*   **Visual Observations**: 
    *   **Single-Seed**: A blue pilot-wave triangle with a single vertical cyan tracer line down the center.
    *   **Random-Seed**: Settles into a completely static barcode of vertical colored lines (purple, pink, blue, yellow-green).
*   **Dynamics**: The waves guide the particles, but the system locks into a static, time-invariant configuration almost immediately.
*   **Aesthetic Value**: Average. The static vertical lines lack dynamic texture.

### Rule 6: Discrete Nonlinear Schrödinger Envelope
*   **Key Mechanism**: Dispersion at low amplitudes and self-focusing at high amplitudes.
*   **Visual Observations**: 
    *   **Single-Seed**: A single, stationary red vertical line in a black vacuum.
    *   **Random-Seed**: A few vertical red stripes of varying widths on a dark background.
*   **Dynamics**: Low-energy waves disperse to zero (black), leaving behind self-focused, localized stationary solitons.
*   **Aesthetic Value**: Minimalist. It is physically fascinating but visually simple.

### Rule 7: Fractional Chiral Edge States (Winner)
*   **Key Mechanism**: Conservation of fractional charges (+1/3, +2/3, +1, -1/3, -2/3, -1) with chiral propagation.
*   **Visual Observations**: 
    *   **Single-Seed**: Exceptionally clean, sharp diagonal lines (blue, light blue, peach) that cancel or shift when colliding.
    *   **Random-Seed**: A dense network of intersecting laser-like lines at the top that filters out all left-movers, leaving only a clean, parallel set of orange lines at the bottom.
*   **Dynamics**: Exact charge conservation. Collisions act as perfect subtractive filters, leading to mutual annihilation.
*   **Aesthetic Value**: Outstanding. The thin, glowing lines against the black background create a minimalist, high-tech vector aesthetic.

### Rule 8: Beam Splitter Interferometer (Winner)
*   **Key Mechanism**: Static mirrors, phase-shifters, and beam splitters that manipulate moving wave packets.
*   **Visual Observations**: 
    *   **Single-Seed**: A green wave goes down-right, passes through a yellow phase-shifter (turning cyan), reflects off a grey mirror (turning magenta), and passes through the phase-shifter again (turning orange).
    *   **Random-Seed**: A gorgeous, intricate lattice of zigzagging colored paths bouncing off mirrors and phase-shifters.
*   **Dynamics**: Clean, deterministic wave reflections and phase changes, forming a complex optical circuit.
*   **Aesthetic Value**: Outstanding. The contrast of the bright, colored zigzag paths (green, cyan, magenta, orange) against the black background and static components makes it look like an active quantum optical processor.

### Rule 9: Schrödinger Wave-Packet Dispersion
*   **Key Mechanism**: Spatial Laplacian coupling of real and imaginary wave function components.
*   **Visual Observations**: 
    *   **Single-Seed**: An expanding diamond filled with an extremely fine, high-frequency checkerboard pattern of blue and red.
    *   **Random-Seed**: A uniform background of fine vertical blue/violet lines with minor orange transients.
*   **Dynamics**: Free dispersion of the wave packet, showing rapid oscillations.
*   **Aesthetic Value**: Very good. The single-seed cone has a unique shimmering, holographic look.

### Rule 10: Chiral Solitonic Rabi Oscillations
*   **Key Mechanism**: Atoms absorb pump waves, excite, and undergo stimulated emission.
*   **Visual Observations**: 
    *   **Single-Seed**: Two large colored wedges (green, magenta) that meet in the center; the bottom half is blank black.
    *   **Random-Seed**: Virtually entirely black, with only a few colored pixels at the very top.
*   **Dynamics**: Rapid excitation followed by stimulated emission and complete decay to ground state (vacuum).
*   **Aesthetic Value**: Poor/average due to the rapid decay and lack of long-term structure.

### Detailed Justifications

### 1. Rule 8: Beam Splitter Interferometer
*   **Visual Appeal**: This rule produces an incredibly intricate, multicolored lattice. The single-seed diagram is a textbook demonstration of optical manipulation, showing wave-packet propagation, phase shifting (color change), and mirror reflection (direction and phase change).
*   **Complex Dynamics**: In the random-seed run, the space-time diagram acts as a complex interferometer circuit. The waves interact dynamically with the static mirrors (grey), phase-shifters (yellow), and beam splitters (white). Destructive interference cancels opposing waves, creating empty lanes next to densely populated zigzag highways.
*   **Aesthetics**: The high contrast of neon green, cyan, magenta, and orange lines bouncing between static circuit elements against a deep navy background creates a stunning visual that resembles a futuristic optoelectronic chip or a laser light show.

### 2. Rule 3: Discrete Wave Equation
*   **Visual Appeal**: Unlike standard CAs which often generate fractal triangles or chaotic noise, this rule generates a clean, rectangular plaid or tartan pattern.
*   **Complex Dynamics**: The Euler discretization of the wave equation with clipping leads to flat, stable regions of displacement and velocity. When waves propagate in both directions, they form a rectilinear grid of intersecting colored bars. The clipping function ensures that the waves do not blow up, creating sharp boundaries between states.
*   **Aesthetics**: The resulting space-time diagram looks like a carefully designed modernist textile or a digital glitch art canvas. The bright, contrasting blocks of orange, cyan, purple, and magenta create a highly ordered, geometric harmony.

### 3. Rule 7: Fractional Chiral Edge States
*   **Visual Appeal**: This rule is characterized by extremely clean, thin, single-pixel-wide diagonal lines that cut through a black background.
*   **Complex Dynamics**: It models exact charge conservation. Right-moving positive charges and left-moving negative charges collide and subtract algebraically. In the random-seed diagram, this leads to a fascinating "filtering" effect: the upper portion shows many collisions and crossings, but as time progresses, the opposing charges cancel each other out, leaving only a set of parallel right-moving orange lines that exit the system.
*   **Aesthetics**: The visual signature is clean, sleek, and minimalist. The transition from a busy grid of collisions at the top to quiet, parallel "edge channels" at the bottom provides a satisfying visual narrative of order emerging from interaction.
